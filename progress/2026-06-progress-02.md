# Progress Update #2 (June 2026)

**Period covered:** March 2026 to June 2026

## 1) Summary
Since the March update, NurseLink has moved from an early B2C home nursing marketplace with a NUHS-first B2B build into a clearer dual-track model:

- **B2C:** Continue helping families arrange home nursing quickly through the app, website, WhatsApp and manual fulfilment.
- **B2B:** Build care operations software for home care providers, assisted living operators, community care partners and eventually hospital-at-home workflows.

The main strategic shift is that NurseLink is no longer relying only on NUHS or large hospital partnerships as the near-term path. Based on stakeholder feedback, smaller private care providers, assisted living operators and community/community-care partners may be faster and more realistic initial customers.

## 2) Objectives recap
- Scale B2C adoption with stronger fulfilment and lower-friction intake.
- Build a configurable B2B care operations platform, starting with home care, assisted living and community care workflows.
- Improve trust, quality assurance, nurse verification, escalation processes and compliance readiness.
- Convert early conversations into pilots, recurring care arrangements or paying B2B users.

## 9) Compliance, trust and quality assurance
- Stakeholder feedback strongly reinforced that NurseLink cannot operate like a simple matching marketplace.
- Key risks to address:
  - nurse verification
  - practising certificate and registration checks
  - scope of practice
  - liability insurance
  - escalation and backup support
  - quality assurance
  - clinical governance
  - clear distinction between caregiver/HCA support and RN-led care
- Community partners may require a clearer explanation of how NurseLink ensures care quality beyond checking certificates.
- Compliance roadmap direction:
  - **Phase 1:** private organisations, assisted living, nursing homes and home care providers with PDPA baseline.
  - **Phase 2:** community care partners and stronger reporting / governance workflows.
  - **Phase 3:** deeper healthcare or hospital workflows only after stronger compliance, credibility and operational proof.
